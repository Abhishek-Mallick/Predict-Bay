Basic Questions
1. What is the stock market?

The stock market is a place where publicly traded companies' stocks or shares are bought and sold. Investors purchase stocks in the hope of making a profit, either by selling them at a higher price or by earning dividends on their investment.

2. How does the stock market work?

Companies issue stocks when they want to raise capital, or money, to fund their operations or expansion plans. These stocks are then traded on stock exchanges, which are platforms where buyers and sellers can trade stocks. The price of a stock is determined by supply and demand. If there are more buyers than sellers, the price of the stock goes up. If there are more sellers than buyers, the price of the stock goes down.

3. What is a Closing Price?

The closing price is reported by stock exchanges at the end of each trading day and is widely available through financial news outlets, online trading platforms, and other financial resources. It is important for investors to keep track of the closing price of stocks they are interested in to make informed investment decisions.
