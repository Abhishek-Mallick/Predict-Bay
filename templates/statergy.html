TURTLE STATERGY | {{ticker}}
DESCRIPTION
The Turtle strategy, also known as the Turtle Trading strategy, is a trend-following trading strategy developed by Richard Dennis and William Eckhardt in the 1980s. It gained popularity when a group of novice traders, known as the "Turtles," achieved significant success using this strategy. The Turtle strategy is based on the idea of riding long-term trends in the market. It is a systematic approach that uses technical indicators to determine entry and exit points for trades. The strategy aims to capture substantial profits from trending markets while also implementing strict risk management rules. Here are the key principles and components of the Turtle strategy: Breakout Entry: The Turtle strategy identifies breakouts, which occur when the price surpasses a certain range or level of support/resistance. The strategy utilizes a Donchian channel, which consists of the highest high and lowest low over a specific period. When the price breaks above the channel's upper boundary, it triggers a long entry, and when it breaks below the lower boundary, it triggers a short entry. Position Sizing: The Turtle strategy emphasizes the concept of position sizing based on volatility. It uses the Average True Range (ATR) indicator to determine the appropriate position size for each trade. The position size is typically a percentage of the trader's equity, adjusted according to the market's volatility. Stop Loss and Take Profit: The strategy incorporates dynamic stop loss and take profit levels based on the recent market volatility. The initial stop loss is set at a certain multiple of the ATR away from the entry price, providing a predefined risk level for each trade. The take profit level is usually not predefined but is instead trailed along with the trend, allowing for potential large gains during extended trends. Pyramiding: The Turtle strategy utilizes a pyramiding technique, where additional positions are added as the trade moves in the desired direction. Each new position is initiated at predetermined price intervals or levels, allowing the trader to increase exposure and potential profits during favorable market conditions. Trend Identification: The strategy aims to identify and trade strong, sustained trends. It uses long-term moving averages, such as the 50-day or 200-day moving average, to determine the overall trend direction. Trades are taken only in the direction of the prevailing trend, avoiding counter-trend trades. Systematic Approach: The Turtle strategy is a rule-based and systematic approach, aiming to remove subjective decision-making from trading. Traders follow the predefined rules strictly, regardless of personal opinions or emotions, to maintain consistency and discipline. Portfolio Diversification: To mitigate risk, the Turtle strategy recommends diversifying the trading portfolio across different markets and asset classes. This approach helps to spread the risk and reduces the reliance on any single trade or market. Overall, the Turtle strategy is designed to capture and ride long-term trends while managing risk through strict position sizing, stop loss levels, and systematic rules. It focuses on disciplined execution, risk management, and adherence to the predefined strategy rather than making subjective judgments based on market sentiment or opinions.
